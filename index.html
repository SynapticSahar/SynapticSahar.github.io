About

Where Neuroscience
Meets Machine Intelligence

I'm a master's student in Computational Neuroscience at Pompeu Fabra University, working at the intersection of brain dynamics and deep learning. My question: how does the brain maintain the perfect balance between stability and chaos — and can we teach machines to do the same?

My thesis with Prof. Gustavo Deco and Dr. Ruben Moreno-Bote measures whole-brain metastability from fMRI using a novel vortex-space entropy approach, connecting it to the Maximum Occupancy Principle and predicting human cognitive flexibility.

Before this, I built deep learning models for wearable EEG/EMG devices at Myant Inc., and developed a threshold-free SWR detection pipeline using transformers. I write production-ready code and deploy full ML systems end-to-end.

📍 Barcelona, Spain  ·  🇬🇧 English fluent  ·  🇮🇷 Persian native

Active Research · 2025–Present

Brain Metastability & the Maximum Occupancy Principle

Measuring how flexibly the brain navigates metastable states from fMRI (HCP · 1000 subjects). Computing Shannon & path entropy in vortex-space to quantify cognitive flexibility, correlating with decision-making performance.

Core hypothesis: entropy of brain state occupancy reflects the MOP optimum — the brain's sweet spot between rigid habituation and uncontrolled chaos.
